About Me

I am currently a Lecturer (equivalent to assistant professor in the US) within School of Computer Science at the University of Bristol and a faculty member of the MAVI group. I am also a member of ELLIS – the European Laboratory for Learning and Intelligent Systems.

Prior to Bristol, I was very lucky to work with Dr. Hakan Bilen as a PhD student (also supervised by Prof. Timothy Hospedales) and a postdoc and spent five wonderful years at the University of Edinburgh. I have also been a postdoctoral fellow within the MultiMedia Lab (MMLab) at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, working with Dr. Xiangyu Yue. Before Edinburgh, I did my master and bachelor at Sun Yat-sen University, working with Prof. Wei-Shi Zheng who thankfully introduced me to computer vision. During the master program, I was lucky to visit Queen Mary University of London to work with Prof. Shaogang Gong FREng.

My research interests are in computer vision and machine learning, with a focus on multi-task/domain learning and enable the visual models to learn from limited human supervision and to be 3D-aware. My PhD thesis is awarded the BMVA Sullivan Doctoral Thesis Prize Runner-Up. My MTPSL paper is awarded the CVPR 2022 Best Paper Nominees. I serve as Area Chair at ICLR'26 and have been a reviewer for top-tier conferences such as CVPR, ICCV, NeurIPS and journals such as TPAMI et al and received the top reviewer award at NeurIPS'23'24.

PhD & Research Opportunities

I am actively seeking highly self-motivated PhD students, Postdoc, visiting researchers, and research interns to join our team at the University of Bristol. We work on exciting areas including:

  • Generative modeling and synthesis
  • 3D computer vision and spatial understanding
  • Motion generation and human-centered AI
  • Multimodal learning and representation
  • Universal representation learning
